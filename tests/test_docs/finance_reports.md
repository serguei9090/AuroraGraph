# Macro-Financial Quarterly Report: Q4 FY2025

## Executive Summary
For the fiscal year ended December 31, 2025, OmniCorp Global reported a consolidated net revenue of $14.2 billion, an 18% year-over-year increase compared to FY2024's $12.03 billion. Earnings Per Share (EPS) diluted climbed from $4.12 to $5.45. Operating margins expanded by 240 basis points to 22.1%, driven primarily by supply chain optimizations in the APAC region and favorable forex tailwinds.

## Segment Performance

### Cloud Services & Infrastructure
Cloud Services remained our fastest-growing segment. Q4 revenue touched $2.8 billion (up 31% YoY). For the full FY2025, cloud revenue was $9.4 billion. Notably, the new "Quantum-Safe Storage" tier reached 14,000 enterprise subscribers within 90 days of launch, generating an annualized run rate (ARR) of $420 million. Operating expenses for this segment were $1.1 billion in Q4, with $400 million directly attributed to GPU cluster depreciation.

### Consumer Hardware
Consumer Hardware faced severe macroeconomic headwinds. Full-year revenue dropped 8% to $3.1 billion. The flagship "OmniPhone 14" shipped only 2.2 million units in Q4, missing the consensus estimate of 2.8 million units. The gross margin for the hardware division shrank from 34% to 29%, largely due to a 15% increase in rare-earth metal costs (specifically Neodymium and Dysprosium) and a 10% tariff hike on imported silicon wafers enacted in October.

### AI Enterprise Solutions
Our B2B AI software suite "OmniMind" achieved remarkable profitability. With a customer acquisition cost (CAC) of $12,500 and a customer lifetime value (LTV) of $185,000, the LTV:CAC ratio reached an industry-leading 14.8x. Churn rate dropped to a historic low of 1.2% annually. Segment revenue was $1.7 billion for the year.

## Balance Sheet & Cash Flow
The company generated $4.1 billion in free cash flow (FCF) for FY2025. Cash and cash equivalents at year-end stood at $18.5 billion, while total debt was reduced to $6.2 billion. Interest expenses for the year were $310 million. In November 2025, the Board authorized a new $5 billion share repurchase program and increased the quarterly dividend by 12% to $0.48 per share.

## Forward Guidance (FY2026)
For Q1 FY2026, management expects revenue between $3.4 billion and $3.6 billion, with non-GAAP EPS between $1.25 and $1.30. For the full FY2026 year, we are projecting total revenue of $16.5 billion to $17.0 billion, representing approximately 16% to 20% YoY growth. Capital expenditures (CapEx) are expected to peak at $3.5 billion as we build out two new sustainable data centers in Reykjavik, Iceland, and Santiago, Chile.
